# 2026-04-21

Conducted a nine-point fact-check of an LLM-generated "2026 blueprint" pitch proposing Polymarket and related prediction-market integrations for a UK-resident hobbyist operator. Primary-source research (Polymarket docs, Kalshi member agreement Oct 2025, GitHub, HMRC guidance via Freshfields/Koinly/Deloitte, arxiv) found that three of the pitch's core claims are outright contradicted: the UK geoblock is KYC-enforced (not IP-only), Kalshi explicitly lists the UK as a Restricted Jurisdiction in its October 2025 member agreement, and USDC-denominated prediction-market winnings attract CGT with no gambling exemption. Two further claims — maker-rebate profitability and arbitrage window persistence — are contradicted by empirical data (rebate pools collapsed; 73% of arb profits captured by sub-100ms bots). PMXT SDK exists and is real (1.6k stars, v2.31.4 as of today) but is TypeScript-first and has limited utility while UK trading venues remain blocked. ForecastEx via Interactive Brokers is the only prediction-market-adjacent venue confirmed accessible to UK residents, with a narrow US-economic-indicator market scope. Decision: existing project invariant (Polymarket out of scope) stands and is now better-evidenced. Created [[20-Markets/Polymarket-Feasibility-2026]] with full sourced analysis.
